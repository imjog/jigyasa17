ABOUT

The Annual Physics convention,JIGYASA is to be the meeting point of curious minds gathered in a common pursuit of knowledge. Following the legacy, JIGYASA'17 aims to provide a platform and share ideas and enlighten the participant about modern vistas of thought and technology. Along with two days of guest lectures by eminent physicists, we also bring up a bunch of exciting competitions like debate , quiz and much more. All one need is to -- STAY CURIOUS..

PREVIOUS GUEST LECTURES

Prof. Ashoke Sen

Padma Shri, Padma Bhushan, Dirac Medal awardee and a member of Royal Society nominated by Stephen Hawking himself, Ashoke Sen is a theoretical physicist and distinguished professor at HRI, Allahabad. His main area of research is String Theory.

Dr. J.N. Goswami

Dr. J.N. Goswami was a lead in Chandrayaan-1 mission. He received the INSA medal for Young Scientist(1978), the NASA Public Service Group Achievement Award(1986) and also the S.S. Bhatnagar award(1994).

Prof. Ajoy Ghatak

Prof. Ajoy Ghatak is a famous Indian physicist, an awardee of the S.S. Bhatnagar Prize for Science and Technology in Physical Sciences. He has written over 170 research papers and over 20 books ranging from Quantum Physics to Special Relativity and Fiber Optics in which he excels.

  • Prof. Apoorva D. Patel

    Prof. Apoorva D. Patel is widely known for his work in applying concepts of quantum algorithms and quantum information theory to understand the structure of genetic languages. His major contribution has been in the field of quantum chromodynamics. After his PhD at Caltech, he served as the Scientific Associate at CERN, Geneva and then joined IISc, Bangalore as a faculty member in 1989. His research interests include a wide range of topics such as lattice gauge theories, renormalisation group, light-front QCD, quark-gluon plasma, quantum computation, languages of genes and proteins and black hole thermodynamics.

  • Ritu Karidhal

    Known as “The Woman Behind Mangalyaan”, Ritu Karidhal was the Deputy Operations Director for to India’s Mars orbital mission, Mangalyaan. She played a major role in dealing with the detailing and the execution of the craft's onward autonomy system. She has worked on many other ISRO projects earlier and served as Operations Director for some of these. As a scientist at ISRO, she is currently working on the Chandrayaan 2 mission. She is the recipient of the ISRO Young Scientist Award in 2007 from A. P. J. Abdul Kalam, then president of India.

  • Prof. Debashish Chowdhury

    Prof. Debashish Chowdhury is a renowned physicist working on a wide variety of topics in Statistical and Biological Physics. He is currently the Head of Department of Physics at IIT, Kanpur. He is a member of the Board of Editors of European Physical Journal B, managing editor of the International Journal of Modern Physics C and a former Associate Member of ICTP (Italy). He is an elected Fellow of Indian National Science Academy (New Delhi), Indian Academy of Sciences (Bangalore) and National Academy of Sciences (Allahabad).

  • Prof. Sanjeev Dhurandhar

    Prof. Sanjeev Dhurandhar who is currently an Emeritus Professor at Inter-University Centre for Astronomy and Astrophysics (IUCAA), Pune was part of the Indian team which contributed to the detection of gravitational waves. He is a Science advisor to the IndIGO consortium council and has helped ensure India will get a LIGO of its own in the mid-2020s. He is one of the scientists who won the Special Breakthrough Prize in Fundamental Physics 2016 and alsorecieved the Bhushan H.K. Firodia award in 2016.

  • Dr. Ujjwal Sen

    Dr. Ujjwal Sen is an Associate Professor and a member of the Quantum Information and Computing group at the Harish-Chandra Research Institute. Apart from quantum information and computing, his research interests include quantum communication and cryptography, condensed matter physics and quantum optics. He is the recipient of Kusuma Memorial Outstanding Young Faculty Award at IIT Delhi, the Ramon-y-Cajal fellowship and the Humboldt fellowship.

PARIPRACHH

RULES

• Participants have to team up in groups of two.
•There will be two rounds. The prelims will be in a written format where each team would be given a sheet and a number of questions would be asked. Based on the number of correct answers teams will be shortlisted for the finals.
•The selected teams will advance to the finals, where there will be multiple rounds having different styles of questions.
•There will also be audience participation in the finals. We will need to give small gifts to the audience for correct responses.
•The winners will be decided from the finals.
•The whole event structure will be designed in consultation with the Quiz Club, lIT (BHU) who will be responsible for the entire content of the quiz.
•The questions asked will mostly be from the field of Physics.

RULES

TIER 1

•Each participant will deliver a two minute speech.
•Out of the given topics, the final topic will be announced five minutes before the contestant speaks.
•The best 8 participants will be selected for tier 2.

TIER 2

•The finalists will be divided into teams of 4.
•30-40 minute’s debate will be held between the teams.
•Each speaker will put forward his/her arguments and counter the arguments of the previous speakers. To ensure equal opportunity, the first speaker will be given a chance at the end.
•A speaker can be questioned while he/she is speaking, only for further justification of his/her argument.
•A speaker could also be asked a ‘point of information’ related to what he/she has just spoken.
•The first speaker may take time to introduce the motion and last speaker to conclude the motion.
•The topic for tier 2 will be one from the list of topics provided.
